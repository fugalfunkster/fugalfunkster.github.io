NOTES

I am a voracious learner and I relish taking meticulous notes. My notes not only help me organize my thoughts, they help me persist those thoughts while I direct my focus to the next frontier. Instead of relying on documentation, I rely on my org docs. Within emacs' org-mode, I build interactive outlines, create dynamic todo lists, and play with inline code blocks that I can execute on the fly or programmatically extract into source files. My notes put the literate in literate programming.

Like a good junior wizard, all of my notes, todos, and configs are under version control. And, in the open source spirit, I offer my notes to the curious:

ETHOS

My name is Matthew Hoselton. I was a 6-year-old tech visionary, who let life become an adventure.

I've studied stem-cell biology, avant-garde music performance, and law. I've worked for political campaigns, environmental non-profits, and an organic fertilizer distributor. Now, I garden, bike, and write software in beautiful Portland, Oregon.

I design resilient systems for farms and small businesses, build tools to achieve management goals, and make things happen.

Solving problems begins with mindfulness. I help people articulate their needs and craft goals to meet those needs.

Robust systems are composed of simple components. I write future-ready software through functional modularity.

Organization is a mindset. I practice agile and lean techniques, master powerful tools, and keep projects moving forward.
